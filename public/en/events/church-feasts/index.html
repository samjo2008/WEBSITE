The Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church follows one of the most ancient and spiritually rich calendar systems in the world — a tradition inherited from the early Church in Egypt and harmonized with the Coptic Calendar.

Key Calendar Facts

  • Days per Year: 365 days.
  • Leap Year: An additional day is added every fourth year.
  • Evangelist Cycle: Each year is dedicated to one of the Four Evangelists — Matthew, Mark, Luke, and John. Luke’s year is the Ethiopian Leap Year.
  • Months: 12 months of 30 days each.
  • Pagumen: A 13th month of 5 days (6 in a leap year).

Monthly Feasts of the Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church

The Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church observes a vast number of feast days throughout the year. The Annual Feast is marked by a procession of the Tabot (Ark), and each month carries its own rhythm of remembrance, honoring saints, angels, and sacred events.

Chronology & Movable Feasts

The chronology of the Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church is counted from the Birth of Our Lord Jesus Christ, known as the Era of Incarnation. Because of this ancient reckoning, the Ethiopian calendar differs from the Gregorian calendar by 7 or 8 years, depending on the month.

The calculation of Easter (Fasika) — the center of all movable feasts — follows the traditional system of Ammonius, a method preserved by the Church for centuries.

Rules for Movable Feasts
  • Easter Lent: Begins on a Monday (between Yekatit 1 – Megabit 5).
  • Palm Sunday: Between Megabit 19 – Miyazia 23.
  • Easter (Fasika): Always a Sunday (between Megabit 26 – Miyazia 30).
  • Ascension: Always a Thursday.
  • Pentecost: Always a Sunday.

Religious Festivals (Ba’alat)

The Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church celebrates a rich tapestry of religious feasts, honoring the life of Our Lord, the Virgin Mary, the Apostles, Angels, Righteous Saints, and Martyrs. These feasts guide the faithful through a sacred rhythm of worship, remembrance, and thanksgiving.

The Senksar — the Book of Saints — lists the commemorations for each day of the year, ensuring that the Church continually remembers the holy men and women who lived in faith.

9 Major Feasts of Our Lord
  • His Conception
  • Christmas (Lidet / Gena)
  • Epiphany (Timket)
  • Transfiguration
  • Palm Sunday (Hosanna)
  • Good Friday
  • Easter (Fasika)
  • Ascension
  • Pentecost
Feasts of the Virgin Mary

The Church commemorates 33 feasts of the Holy Virgin Mary, Mother of God. Among these, two are especially notable:

  • Monthly: The 21st day of every Ethiopian month.
  • Kidane Mehret: Yekatit 16 (February 24 Gregorian).

Fixed National Feasts

The Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church celebrates several fixed feasts each year. These feasts occur on the same Ethiopian calendar date annually and hold deep spiritual significance for the faithful. Below is a list of the major fixed national feasts and their corresponding Gregorian dates.

Fasting and Abstinence

Fasting is a central pillar of the Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church’s spiritual life. Across the year, the faithful observe numerous fasts that cultivate discipline, humility, repentance, and deeper communion with God. There are approximately 250 fast days annually, with about 180 being obligatory for believers above the age of 13.

General Fasting Rules
  • Frequency: One meal a day, taken in the evening or after 2:45 p.m.
  • Diet: Abstention from meat, fats, eggs, and dairy; meals consist of cereals and vegetables.
  • Exemptions: No fasting during Christmas, Epiphany, and the 50 days between Easter and Pentecost.
Major Obligatory Fasts
  • Great Lent (ዐብይ ነነዊ / ጾመ፡): 56 days.
  • Fast of the Apostles (ጾመ ነቢያት): 10–40 days (begins after Pentecost).
  • Fast of the Prophets / Advent Fast (ጾመ ነቢያት): 40 days before Christmas.
  • Fast of the Assumption of the Virgin Mary (ጾመ ፍልሰተ): 16 days.
  • Fast of Nineveh (ጾመ ነነዊ): 3 days (pre‑Lent).
  • Gahad (ገሐድ ጾም): Eves of Christmas and Epiphany.
  • Fast of Wellness / Fast of Salvation (ጾመ ዕንቁ፡): Every Wednesday & Friday (except during the 50 days after Easter).
